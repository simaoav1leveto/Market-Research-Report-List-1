<p><p>The Smart Coffee Maker market is primarily divided into two types: Wi-Fi-enabled and Bluetooth-enabled coffee makers. Wi-Fi-enabled smart coffee makers connect to home networks, allowing users to control brewing remotely via smartphone apps, schedule brews, and integrate with smart home systems. Bluetooth-enabled smart coffee makers, on the other hand, connect directly to devices over short distances, enabling users to customize settings and monitor brewing via an app, but lacking the extensive control offered by Wi-Fi connectivity.</p></p>
